Old Pal

Description

The old pal is a cocktail originally made with rye whiskey, French vermouth (dry), and Campari. It is similar to a Negroni, but with rye whiskey instead of gin and dry vermouth instead of sweet. Harry MacElhone, the proprietor of Harry's New York Bar in Paris, France, is recognized as the first to publish the cocktail's recipe. MacElhone named this cocktail after William "Sparrow" Robinson, a sports editor for The New York Herald in Paris. This three-ingredient cocktail is essentially a drier version of the boulevardier, which was also invented by MacElhone.

Build

  • 1.5oz Rye Whiskey
  • 1oz Sweet Vermouth
  • 1oz Campari

Execution

  1. Add all ingredients to a mixing glass
  2. Top with ice and stir until chilled and diluted

Serving

Serve in a coupe, express lemon peel over drink, and garnish on rim.
